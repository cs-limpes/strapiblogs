About Me

My Background in Technology

...and How I Got Here

My journey into the world of technology began in 1994, sparked by a horrifying life-altering event. After my mother became a quadrilateral amputee and spent several months in rehabilitation, the state of California provided us with an IBM 386 running Windows 3.1 on DOS 6.0. This machine wasn't just a piece of technology - it became a gateway to a new world for us. Even though my mother faced the challenges of using a computer, and the entirety of life forever, without limbs, it opened up possibilities that were previously unimaginable for my entire family - but especially for me. Without any reservation whatsoever, I can say that this completely changed my life - I would lose my mother a few years later, but the technology that was gifted to our family would change the path of life entirely.

This computer, prone to frequent breakdowns, introduced me to the realm of tech support, IT troubleshooting, and computer programming. A kind tech support specialist took me under her wing, and before I knew it, I was in love with the field. I built my own 8088 from spare parts and took my first steps into becoming the software engineer, architect, and consultant I am today.

Formal Classes and Self Teaching

My high school had just introduced new computer programming curricula where I learned Basic, Pascal, and C++, and Microsoft Office. After high school, HTML and CSS became my tools of choice, thanks to GeoCities, and I ventured into JavaScript and PHP, crafting websites for anyone who needed them.

My college didn't offer a computer science program and I pursued dual majors in Psychology/Philosophy, a decision that didn't deter my tech journey. My fascination with digital landscapes only grew, leading me to freelance website development, SEO, and digital marketing, marking my pivot from a corporate healthcare accreditation career to embracing my true calling.

A Holistic Approach

Website development used to encompass SEO, Digital Marketing, and graphic design and since I started creating websites when websites were first being developed, I learned these skills as well. Understanding the relationship between technical mechanics, user experience, SEO, and content marketing has been central to my strategy, ensuring success for the clients I work with.
